Slicer Setup & Kintsugi Guide

KintsugiVase allows you to "splice" two G-codes together. Print a dense, heavy, perfectly solid base (for stability, complex internal geometry like threads, or water-tightness) and instantly transition into a seamless, fast, single-wall spiral vase for the upper aesthetics.

Mandatory Slicer Settings

1. Enable "Verbose G-code"

This tool requires exact Z-height tracking. Without Verbose G-code, it cannot read the layers.

  • OrcaSlicer / Bambu Studio: Go to Others tab -> G-code output -> Verbose G-code (Check the box).
  • PrusaSlicer: Go to Print Settings -> Output options -> Verbose G-code.

2. Aligned Seams & Concentric Tops

  • Seam Position: Set to Aligned or Back for both files. This prevents the nozzle from dragging across the print during the transition.
  • Top Surface Pattern: Set the Base file's Top Surface Pattern to Concentric. This forces the solid infill to perfectly trace the outer wall, eliminating any tiny gaps when the vase wall starts printing on top of it.

Ideal Project Types

  • Threaded & Complex Fittings: Print a 100% solid base with fully functional, complex internal geometry (like threaded pipe fittings) that transitions seamlessly into a lightweight single-wall structure above.
  • Planters: Solid, water-tight bases with infill to hold heavy soil.

CAD Design Tips

  • Continuous Loops: At your chosen Z-transition height, your CAD model's outer profile must be a single continuous shape (no holes or separated islands).
  • Avoid Sharp Overhangs: Transitioning from solid infill directly into a steep outward overhang in single-wall mode can cause curling.

Using the App

  • Export the Base: Export your standard Base file. Upload it here. The app will scan it. Use the Z-slider to find the exact height where you want the vase to begin.
  • Export the Vase: Go back to your slicer, turn on "Spiral Vase Mode", slice, and export. Do not move the model on the build plate between exports!
  • Wall Width Adjustment: Single-wall prints often need thicker walls for strength. Use the explicit Target Vase Width input to digitally inflate your vase wall size automatically!

Warning & Disclaimer

Generating and executing custom, spliced G-code can result in unexpected toolhead movements.

Use this tool entirely at your own risk. The creator assumes absolutely no responsibility for any damage to your 3D printer, failed prints, wasted filament, or any other negative outcomes resulting from the use of this software.

Please refer to the Setup Guide and always closely supervise your 3D printer during the transition layers when testing new files.

Tip: Export files as .gcode.3mf to preserve thumbnails for Bambu LAN printing.
